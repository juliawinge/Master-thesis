# In-Silico PCR to detect potential non-specific amplification.
*This step describes how to use UCSC In-Silico PCR to identify potential non-specific amplification products before downstream testing.*

## 1. Run In Silico PCR
Go to UCSC In-Silico PCR:
https://genome.ucsc.edu/cgi-bin/hgPcr <br>

Paste the forward and reverse sequences into respective fields.  <br>

Click **submit**.  <br>

## 2. Evaluate the PCR products
Ideally, only a single PCR product corresponding to the intended target region should be detected. <br>

If a **fix** option appears for the same chromosome and target region, it is generally not considered problematic. <br>

If additional PCR products are detected outside the intended target region, particularly on other chromosomes, the primer pair should be considered non-specific and redesigned.  <br>
