# Design target assays
*This step describes how to design candidate primer pairs for target sites that have passed the BLAT specificity assessment. The goal is to generate short amplicons suitable for fragmented DNA while minimizing the risk of off-target amplification.*

## Requirements
Before starting this step, the target site should have been evaluated according to **7. Check for pseudogenes and highly similar sequences using BLAT**. <br>

The following information should be available:
* Target site position
* Target sequence
* Region identified as unique and suitable for primer placement
* Regions identified as highly similar and unsuitable for primer placement

## 1. Open Primer-BLAST 
Go to Primer-BLAST:
https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/tools/primer-blast/ <br>

Paste the target sequence obtained in the previous step.

## 2. Define primer search regions
The target site should be included within the amplicon, together with a safety margin of at least 5 bases on each side. <br>

Example:
- Target site: 106-107
- Target interval including safety margin: 100-113 <br>

Forward primers should be placed upstream of the target interval and reverse primers downstream of the target interval.

- Forward primer: <100 
- Reverse primer: >113 <br>

Regions identified as highly similar by BLAT should be avoided whenever possible.<br> 

Example of regions suitable for placement:
```
1-86
94-99
127-129
152-168
```

## 4. Evaluate primer specificity
Review the suggested primer pairs. <br>

Hover over each primer pair to determine its position within the target sequence. <br>

Compare the primer positions with the unique intervals identified during the BLAT analysis. <br>

## 5. Assess 3´anchoring
At least one primer should have a unique 3 ´ end. <br>

A primer was considered acceptable when the last three bases at the 3´ end were located in a sequence region unique to the target. <br>

Example: 
- Forward primer: 79-96
- Positions 94-96 are unique <br>
-> aceptable 3´anchoring <br> <br>

- Reverse primer: 126-145
- Positions 127-129 are unique, but not position 126, which is the last base at the 3´ end. <br>
-> weaker 3´anchoring <br>

## 6. Select candidate primer pairs
<img width="1415" height="139" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/258e4f7e-7909-4b77-8198-a13858a11831" /> <br>

Primer pairs were ranked according to the following criteria:
1. Unique 3 ´ anchoring
2. Low self-3 ´ complementarity
3. Low self-complementarity
4. Similar GC content and melting temperature
5. Short amplicon size

Because the assays were intended for multiplex PCR, primers with similar characteristics were preferred to enable simultaneous amplification of multiple targets. <br>

Short amplicons were prioritised to improve performance with fragmented DNA. <br>

Select the three most promising primer pairs for downstream testing.
